We present \textbf{ResBridge}, a heteroscedastic stochastic bridge on $SE(3)^L$ for RNA backbone ensemble generation. Below we introduce the problem setup, the deterministic SE(3) flow matching backbone, the ResBridge framework itself, and its B-factor-guided variant ResBridge-guided.



\subsection{Problem Definition}

% Two aspects should be explained clearly: 1. The objective of our model (mathematical formulation); 2. The representation of RNA (structure and flexibility).

Given an RNA sequence $s$ of length $L$, we aim to learn a conditional distribution $p_\theta(G\mid s)$ over backbone conformations whose i.i.d.\ samples $\{G^{(k)}\}_{k=1}^K$ satisfy two requirements: (i) accuracy --- physically valid, native-like backbone and (ii) multi-modal coverage --- the ensemble covers multiple conformational states. Following prior RNA backbone modeling~\cite{anand2025rna}, each backbone is represented as a sequence of rigid frames, one per nucleotide, $G=\{(x_i, R_i)\}_{i=1}^L \in SE(3)^L$. Here $SE(3)=\mathbb{R}^3\rtimes SO(3)$ is the Lie group of 3D rigid-body motions, each element of which pairs a translation with a rotation: $x_i\in\mathbb{R}^3$ is the coordinate of the frame origin (the $C4'$ atom) and $R_i\in SO(3)\subset SE(3)$ is the local backbone orientation defined by the three frame atoms $C4'$, $O4'$, and $C3'$. The full RNA backbone thus lives on the product manifold $SE(3)^L$.

% \paragraph{Flexibility.} We represent flexibility as a per-residue non-negative vector $b\in\mathbb{R}^L_{\geq 0}$, where $b_i$ is the crystallographic B-factor of the $C4'$ atom of residue $i$. Under the standard harmonic interpretation, $b_i$ is proportional to the mean-squared displacement of atom $i$ around its equilibrium position,
% \begin{equation}
%   b_i \;=\; \tfrac{8\pi^2}{3}\,\mathbb{E}_{G\sim p_{\mathrm{data}}(\cdot\mid s)}\!\left[\|x_i - \bar{x}_i\|^2\right], \qquad \bar{x}_i := \mathbb{E}_{G\sim p_{\mathrm{data}}(\cdot\mid s)}[x_i],
% \end{equation}
% so that a dataset of solved structures with experimentally measured B-factors $b^*$ provides second-order supervision on the target distribution. Training data therefore consist of triples $(s, G^*, b^*)$ drawn from the PDB, and the learned model is required to match both the native geometry $G^*$ in expectation and the flexibility profile $b^*$ in variance.


\subsection{SE(3) Flow Matching Backbone}\label{sec:se3-fm}

Flow matching~\cite{lipman2022flow,liu2022flow} learns a neural velocity field that continuously transports a tractable prior $p_0$ to the data distribution $p_{\mathrm{data}}$ along a probability path $\{p_t\}_{t\in[0,1]}$. Since we represent RNA backbones as tuples of rigid frames in $SE(3)^L$, we instantiate flow matching directly on this manifold following~\cite{yim2023fast,bose2023se}: the velocity field $v_\theta: SE(3)^L\times[0,1]\to\mathfrak{se}(3)^L$ outputs, at each residue frame $(x_t,R_t)\in SE(3)$, a translational component in $\mathbb{R}^3$ and a rotational component in $T_{R_t}SO(3)\cong\mathfrak{so}(3)$.

For each sample pair $(G_0,G_1)\sim p_0\times p_{\mathrm{data}}$ and time $t\sim\mathcal{U}(0,1)$, we take the conditional path to be the constant-speed geodesic on $SE(3)$ under its bi-invariant metric; differentiating it in $t$ yields the closed-form target velocity
\begin{equation}
u^{x}_{t}=\frac{x_{1}-x_{t}}{1-t},\qquad u^{R}_{t}=\frac{\log(R_{t}^{\top}R_{1})}{1-t}\in T_{R_{t}}SO(3).
\end{equation}
Letting $\hat{v}^{x}_{t}$ and $\hat{v}^{R}_{t}$ denote the two heads of $v_\theta$ that predict these targets, we train by tangent-space regression
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{SE(3)}}(\theta)\;=\;\mathbb{E}_{t,\,G_0,\,G_1}\!\Bigl[\lambda_x\bigl\|\hat{v}^{x}_{t}-u^{x}_{t}\bigr\|_2^{2}\,+\,\lambda_R\bigl\|\hat{v}^{R}_{t}-u^{R}_{t}\bigr\|_{\mathrm{F}}^{2}\Bigr],
\label{eq:fm-loss}
\end{equation}
with $\lambda_x,\lambda_R>0$ balancing the two modalities and $\|\cdot\|_{\mathrm{F}}$ the Frobenius norm on $\mathfrak{so}(3)$.

The deterministic SE(3) flow matching backbone of \S\ref{sec:se3-fm} transports each starting frame configuration $G_0\!\sim\!p_0$ to a single endpoint via an ODE. Under multi-modal data this commits the velocity field to the conditional mean whenever several ground-truth endpoints are consistent with the same $(G_t,s)$, and the noise-free sampler offers no second mechanism to disperse trajectories across distinct basins once one has been committed to. \textbf{ResBridge} addresses both routes by replacing the deterministic conditional path of \S\ref{sec:se3-fm} with a \emph{stochastic bridge} on $SE(3)^L$ that is closed-form, endpoint-strict, and admits an analytically tractable drift, while leaving the loss, the network, and the velocity target of \S\ref{sec:se3-fm} entirely unchanged.

\paragraph{Three properties.}
We need a conditional distribution $p_t(G_t\!\mid\!G_0,G_1)$ that satisfies:
(i) \emph{closed-form sampling}, so that training remains simulation-free;
(ii) \emph{strict endpoint constraints}, $G_{t=0}\!=\!G_0$ and $G_{t=1}\!=\!G_1$ almost surely;
(iii) \emph{analytic drift}, so that the conditional flow matching loss of \S\ref{sec:se3-fm} can supervise the same velocity field on the same network.
Translations are easy --- the standard Euclidean Brownian bridge supplies all three. Rotations are not: the intrinsic Brownian bridge on $SO(3)$ generated by the Laplace--Beltrami operator has no closed-form simulator, since its transition density is the heat kernel and is only available as a series of Wigner $D$-functions, which is what motivates the truncated IGSO(3) approximations used by prior $SO(3)$ diffusion models~\cite{leach2022denoising,yim2023se}. We sidestep this difficulty by \emph{lifting the bridge to the tangent space at $R_0$ and pushing forward through the exponential map}.

\paragraph{Tangent-space-lifted bridge on $SE(3)^L$.}
Per residue $i$, ResBridge defines the conditional sample $G_{i,t}=(x_{i,t},R_{i,t})$ by two independent Brownian bridges, one in $\mathbb{R}^3$ and one in $\mathfrak{so}(3)\!\cong\!\mathbb{R}^3$. The translation channel is the Euclidean bridge
\begin{equation}
x_{i,t}\;=\;(1-t)\,x_{i,0}+t\,x_{i,1}+\sigma_x\sqrt{t(1-t)}\,\varepsilon^{x}_i,
\qquad \varepsilon^{x}_i\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I_3),
\label{eq:trans-bridge}
\end{equation}
with constant width $\sigma_x>0$ and closed-form marginal $x_{i,t}\!\mid\!x_{i,0},x_{i,1}\sim\mathcal{N}\!\bigl((1-t)x_{i,0}+t\,x_{i,1},\,\sigma_x^2\,t(1-t)\,I_3\bigr)$. The rotation channel takes the relative rotation $R_{i,0}\!\to\!R_{i,1}$ in the principal log
\begin{equation}
\omega_i\;=\;\log\!\bigl(R_{i,0}^{\!\top}R_{i,1}\bigr)^{\!\vee}\;\in\;\mathbb{R}^{3}\;\cong\;\mathfrak{so}(3),
\qquad |\omega_i|<\pi,
\label{eq:rel-omega}
\end{equation}
runs an Euclidean Brownian bridge on $\omega_i$ with constant width $\sigma_R>0$,
\begin{equation}
\Omega_{i,t}\;=\;t\,\omega_i+\sigma_R\sqrt{t(1-t)}\,\varepsilon^{R}_i,
\qquad \varepsilon^{R}_i\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I_3),
\label{eq:tangent-bridge}
\end{equation}
and pushes forward to $SO(3)$ via the right action of the exponential map at $R_{i,0}$,
\begin{equation}
R_{i,t}\;=\;R_{i,0}\,\exp\!\bigl(\,\widehat{\Omega_{i,t}}\,\bigr).
\label{eq:rot-bridge}
\end{equation}
The pair \eqref{eq:trans-bridge}--\eqref{eq:rot-bridge} defines the ResBridge conditional distribution on $SE(3)^L$, parameterized by two constant scalar widths: $\sigma_x$ in {\AA} and $\sigma_R$ in radians.

\paragraph{Honest framing.}
Because \eqref{eq:tangent-bridge} is a Gaussian in a chart anchored at $R_{i,0}$, the resulting marginal $p_t(R_{i,t}\!\mid\!R_{i,0},R_{i,1})$ is a \emph{wrapped Gaussian around the geodesic}, not the heat kernel of $SO(3)$; a different choice of base point would give a different conditional law. ResBridge is therefore a \textbf{chart-induced} stochastic bridge rather than an intrinsic Brownian bridge, and we adopt this construction explicitly because it gives Properties (i)--(iii) in closed form. The IGSO(3)-based training prior of \S\ref{sec:se3-fm} keeps $|\omega_i|$ well below the cut locus $\pi$ at every residue and timestep, so the chart artefact never manifests during training; the same holds at inference because the Euler-Maruyama loop only ever exponentiates a small per-step increment, never a full $\omega$. We discuss this trade-off explicitly in the limitations (\S\ref{sec:resbridge-guided}).

\paragraph{Endpoints, conditional law, and drift.}
The construction \eqref{eq:trans-bridge}--\eqref{eq:rot-bridge} admits the following two propositions, proved in Appendix~A.

\textit{Proposition 1 (Endpoint constraint).} Almost surely, $G_{i,0}=(x_{i,0},R_{i,0})$ and $G_{i,1}=(x_{i,1},R_{i,1})$ for every residue $i$.

\textit{Proposition 2 (Closed-form conditional law).} Conditionally on the endpoints, the per-residue marginals factor and are Gaussians in their respective charts:
\begin{equation*}
x_{i,t}\!\mid\!x_{i,0},x_{i,1}\sim\mathcal{N}\!\bigl((1-t)x_{i,0}+t\,x_{i,1},\,\sigma_x^{2}\,t(1-t)\,I_3\bigr),
\quad
\Omega_{i,t}\!\mid\!R_{i,0},R_{i,1}\sim\mathcal{N}\!\bigl(t\,\omega_i,\,\sigma_R^{2}\,t(1-t)\,I_3\bigr),
\end{equation*}
with $R_{i,t}=R_{i,0}\exp(\widehat{\Omega_{i,t}})$ the pushforward of the tangent-space Gaussian.

A direct consequence of Proposition~2 is that the conditional drift on each modality decouples and is \textbf{identical to the deterministic SE(3) flow matching target of \S\ref{sec:se3-fm}}. Differentiating the bridge SDEs in $\mathbb{R}^3$ and in $\mathfrak{so}(3)$ (Appendix~A) yields
\begin{equation}
u^{x}_{i,t}\;=\;\frac{x_{i,1}-x_{i,t}}{1-t},
\qquad
u^{R}_{i,t}\;=\;\frac{\log\!\bigl(R_{i,t}^{\!\top}R_{i,1}\bigr)^{\!\vee}}{1-t}\;\in\;T_{R_{i,t}}SO(3),
\label{eq:bridge-target}
\end{equation}
which is exactly~\eqref{eq:fm-loss}'s regression target. Conditional flow matching theory~\cite{lipman2022flow,tong2024improving} guarantees that any pair of (legal) conditional sampler and conditional target supervises the corresponding marginal velocity field; ResBridge changes only the sampler in $G_t$, so the loss \eqref{eq:fm-loss}, the network $v_\theta$, and the per-modality weights $\lambda_x,\lambda_R$ are all reused verbatim. \textbf{ResBridge introduces no new trainable parameter and no new loss term.}

\paragraph{Centered translation prior.}
The flow matching backbone of \S\ref{sec:se3-fm} was trained on translation samples whose batch-residue mean is approximately zero (the OT-aligned prior of~\cite{yim2023fast}). Injecting i.i.d.\ Gaussian noise into~\eqref{eq:trans-bridge} breaks this calibration: the residue-mean of $\eta^{x}_i:=\sigma_x\sqrt{t(1-t)}\,\varepsilon^{x}_i$ is $O(1/\sqrt{m_R})$ rather than zero, where $m_R$ is the number of resolved residues per sample. We restore exact zero-mean translations by \emph{post-multiply re-centering} the noise across resolved residues:
\begin{equation}
\tilde\eta^{x}_{i}\;=\;\eta^{x}_{i}\,-\,\frac{1}{m_R}\!\!\sum_{j\in\mathcal{R}}\!\eta^{x}_{j},
\qquad
x_{i,t}\;=\;(1-t)x_{i,0}+t\,x_{i,1}+\tilde\eta^{x}_{i},
\label{eq:trans-recenter}
\end{equation}
so that $\sum_{i\in\mathcal{R}}\tilde\eta^{x}_{i}=0$ holds bit-exact for every sample and $v_\theta$ continues to see corrupted batches whose translation distribution matches its training prior. Re-centering is sample-local and preserves the conditional Gaussian structure of \eqref{eq:trans-bridge} up to an $O(1/m_R)$ correction in the per-residue covariance, two orders of magnitude below the experimental B-factor noise floor (Appendix~C). The same construction is applied transparently in \S\ref{sec:resbridge-guided}, where it becomes essential under per-residue heteroscedasticity.

\paragraph{Sampling.}
At inference we replace the ODE Euler step of \S\ref{sec:se3-fm} by an Euler--Maruyama step on $SE(3)^L$. Given the current state $G_{i,t}$, the network produces a clean prediction $\hat{G}_{i,1}=(\hat{x}_{i,1},\hat{R}_{i,1})$, the corresponding drift velocities $\hat{v}^{x}_{i,t}=(\hat{x}_{i,1}-x_{i,t})/(1-t)$ and $\hat{v}^{R}_{i,t}=\log(R_{i,t}^{\!\top}\hat{R}_{i,1})^{\!\vee}/(1-t)$, and the next state is
\begin{equation}
x_{i,t+\Delta t}=x_{i,t}+\hat{v}^{x}_{i,t}\,\Delta t+\sigma_x\sqrt{\Delta t}\,\xi^{x}_i,
\qquad
R_{i,t+\Delta t}=R_{i,t}\,\exp\!\Bigl(\,\widehat{\hat{v}^{R}_{i,t}\,\Delta t+\sigma_R\sqrt{\Delta t}\,\xi^{R}_i}\,\Bigr),
\label{eq:bridge-em}
\end{equation}
with $\xi^{x}_i,\xi^{R}_i\!\sim\!\mathcal{N}(0,I_3)$ independent across residues and timesteps. Iterating $T$ steps from $K$ independent initializations $G_0^{(k)}\!\sim\!p_0$ produces a conformational ensemble $\{G_1^{(k)}\}_{k=1}^{K}$. We use $\sigma_x=2.0$\,{\AA} and $\sigma_R=0.25$\,rad throughout, which match the typical magnitude of the drift residual that the deterministic backbone leaves at $t\!\approx\!0.5$.

\paragraph{Strict containment of the deterministic backbone.}
Setting $\sigma_x=\sigma_R=0$ collapses every Brownian bridge in \eqref{eq:trans-bridge}--\eqref{eq:rot-bridge} onto its mean and turns \eqref{eq:bridge-em} into the deterministic ODE Euler step of \S\ref{sec:se3-fm}. ResBridge therefore \textbf{strictly contains} the SE(3) flow matching backbone, both at training-time corruption and at inference-time integration. As a consequence, any pretrained backbone of the form \eqref{eq:fm-loss} can be retrofitted to ResBridge by changing two scalar widths and a single line of the corruption / sampling loop, without retraining or fine-tuning the drift.
